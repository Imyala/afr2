# Language fluency playbook (3–6 months + long-term growth)

This note turns the in-app roadmap into a practical guide for learners and
content design.

## 1) 3–6 month speaking plan

### Months 1–3 (foundation to conversational momentum)
- Keep a daily loop: **due review → one lesson → input → output**.
- Bias toward high-frequency words and phrase chunks in real situations.
- Build automatic recall through spaced repetition and timed fluency drills.
- Track baseline vs re-test monthly to keep progress measurable.

### Months 4–6 (toward confident fluency)
- Increase conversation density: longer dialogues and topic-specific speaking.
- Add wider reading/listening with mostly-known language plus a small stretch.
- Keep daily retrieval practice so new vocabulary does not decay.
- Expand grammar and sentence frames through production, not passive reading.

## 2) High-impact learning methods to keep in product design

- **Active recall first:** retrieve before checking notes.
- **Interleaving after foundations:** mix skills once basics are stable.
- **Deep processing:** explain patterns/words in your own words ("teach it back").
- **Comprehensible input + pushed output:** both are required for speaking gains.
- **Feedback loops:** short correction cycles and frequent low-stakes speaking.

## 3) Vocabulary growth targets (long-term)

These targets are directional milestones for content planning and learner
guidance:

- ~500 words: survival communication
- ~2,000 words: conversation foundations
- ~5,000 words: strong day-to-day speaking
- **20,000+ words:** broad fluent speaking target
- **50,000+ words:** high-education / advanced speaking range

The app should keep moving learners upward through graded content, spaced review
and sentence-level production work, rather than isolated recognition tasks.
