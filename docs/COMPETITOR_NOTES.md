# Competitor notes — AfrikaansPod101 (Innovative Language)

A review of AfrikaansPod101 to decide what's worth borrowing. Reviewers
generally rate it as a **supplementary** resource rather than a primary way to
become conversational — which matches our read that it's effective for
*exposure* but weak on forced *production* and measurable mastery.

## What they do (and our take)

| Their feature | Our take |
|---|---|
| Large library of native-speaker **audio/video lessons**, each built around a **dialogue** | Strong for listening exposure. Our analogue is the **reading/dialogue** feature with tap-to-hear lines. |
| **Line-by-line audio** breakdown of the dialogue | Good idea — we already do per-line playback in stories. |
| **"Review track"**: audio active-recall (hear word → pause → say it → hear answer) | Worth adopting as a hands-free **audio review mode** (roadmap). Active recall is exactly our philosophy. |
| **Spaced-repetition flashcards** + custom decks | This is the core of *our* engine already, and we gate mastery on production — arguably stronger. |
| **Word of the Day** (a new free word daily) | **Adopted** — see the home screen Word of the Day (offline, drawn from our own vocab, one tap to add it to your reviews). |
| Topical **vocabulary lists** & build-your-own decks | Roadmap: a browsable vocab list per topic with a "star into my review deck" action. |
| **Cultural insights** | We already embed a cultural note in every lesson. |
| PDF cheat sheets, dictionary | Nice-to-have; lower priority for an offline classroom MVP. |

## What we deliberately do differently (our edge)

- **Production-gated mastery + measurable progress** (baseline → 1-month
  re-test). Pod101 is largely consumption; we force recall and prove gains.
- **Fully offline + installable**, built for low-data classrooms.
- **Game economy** (gems, shop, leagues) tuned for daily school-kid habit.

## Important: licensing of their "free" materials

AfrikaansPod101 / Innovative Language content (audio, video, PDFs, Word of the
Day) is **proprietary — © Innovative Language Learning, all rights reserved**.
"Free lifetime account" means *free to access*, **not** free to copy or
redistribute. So we **cannot legally bundle their lessons/audio/PDFs** into
MzansiLingo.

What we *can* do — and have done — is borrow their effective **features/ideas**
(Word of the Day, line-by-line audio, audio review) and build our **own**
content, while sourcing reusable book content from genuinely open,
Creative-Commons-licensed libraries (African Storybook, Book Dash, Nal'ibali).

Sources: AfrikaansPod101 methodology & free-account pages, plus independent
reviews (Language Sherpa, All Language Resources).
